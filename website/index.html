Why/How do Meteor Showers appear


Lissssten

Meteor Showers occur because Earth passes through the debris left over from comets and asteroids. They appear when they enter Earth’s atmosphere at extremely high speeds causing these debris to burn up as they streak across the sky.

Famous Meteor Showers


Leonids

The brightest and most impressive meteor shower which occurs every November. Can produce thousands of meteors at its peak.


Perseids

Associated with the Comet Swift-Tuttle, which takes 133 years to orbit the sun. We pass its orbit during August every year.


Orionids

The Orionid produces meteors from Halley’s comet, which orbits the sun every 75.-76 years. A shower happens every October and can last for a week.
